Micro-data · Macroeconomics · Policy evaluation

Research on income risk, consumption, labor markets, and welfare.

I am an Assistant Professor at the Department of Economics at Southern Methodist University. I received my Ph.D. in Economics from the University of Minnesota. Prior to that, I studied at CEMFI and Universidad Carlos III de Madrid.

My research combines income and spending micro-data with quantitative life-cycle and spatial models to study macroeconomic policy, labor markets, and household welfare. I am especially interested in research questions where rich data and interpretable models can improve policy design, risk measurement, and counterfactual evaluation.
